Preparation

    Ensure that the area is clean and free of impurities. This includes your body, your clothes, and the place of prayer itself.

  1. Perform Ablution if necessary. You must be ritually pure before you pray. If you're not, it's good to perform Wudu (Ablution) before you do. If since your last prayer you have urinated, defecated, farted, bled excessively, fallen a deep sleep lying down, leaned against something, projectile vomited, or fainted, then you should go to the bathroom.

  2. Make sure all of your necessary parts are covered. The nakedness of a man is considered between the navel and knees. While women has to cover her entire body except for the face and palms.

  3. If you are praying in a masjid(mosque), which is recommended, enter quietly while other fellow Muslims might be still praying and you don't want to disturb them because it is considered extremely rude to disturb someone making a prayer. Stand in a free space away from the entrance/exit.

  4. If you are unsure of the cleanliness of your area, spread a mat or cloth on the floor for good measure. This mat (or prayer rug) is very important to the islamic culture.
